## Context

The thesis trains contrastive moralâ†’fable retrieval models (BGE-base, Linq-Mistral) on **MORABLES**, a 709-pair literary dataset. We were offered TF1-EN-3M, a 3M-fable synthetic corpus generated by Llama-3.1-8B, as potential additional training data. Two open questions before integrating:

1. **Lexical-leakage risk:** Since TF1's morals are *seeded* into the LLM prompt before generation, do they appear nearly verbatim in the fable body? If yes, training on TF1 teaches keyword matching, not semantic understanding â€” which would *hurt* MORABLES transfer.
2. **Diversity:** How many distinct morals does the 3M actually cover?

This experiment answers both empirically.

## Method

### Diagnostic pipeline

[`experiments/11_tf1_diagnostic/check_iou.py`](check_iou.py)

1. **Stream** N rows from TF1's HuggingFace train split as K stratified chunks at evenly-spaced offsets across the 3M.
2. **Extract moral** from the `prompt` field via regex `r"-\s*Teaching:\s*(.+?)(?:\n|$)"` â€” the prompts always contain the moral as a labeled bullet (the `tinyfabulist` generator's template).
3. **Compute IoU** (Jaccard) of moral words vs fable words, both with-and-without stopwords. Same tokenizer / stopword list as [`scripts/02_eda.py`](../../scripts/02_eda.py) for apples-to-apples vs MORABLES.
4. **Track unique-morals growth curve** to detect plateau.
5. **Compare** to MORABLES baseline recomputed in the same run.

### Configuration used

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Total rows streamed | 50,000 |
| Chunks | 10 (stride = 280,000 rows) |
| Extraction success | 100.000% (50,000 / 50,000) |
| Tokenization | `\b\w+\b` lowercased |
| Stopword list | 64 words (matches `scripts/02_eda.py`) |

### Reproducibility

## Findings

### Finding 1 â€” TF1 has only 100 unique morals across the entire 3M dataset

The seed pool is **fixed and global**. Every chunk of 5,000 rows, at every offset across the 3M, found exactly the same 100 morals. The cumulative-unique curve plateaus before the first chunk completes and never grows.

![plateau curve](results/runs/20260502_184314/figures/01_plateau.png)

The flat line at 100 is the entire story: stratified sampling across the dataset cannot find more morals because there are no more morals to find. Extrapolating: each of the 100 morals has **~30,000 fables** generated from it (3M / 100).

The distribution within the 100 is roughly flat â€” top moral has 732 occurrences in the 50K sample, bottom has 337 (~2Ã— spread):

![top morals](results/runs/20260502_184314/figures/03_moral_frequency.png)

The same morals dominate every chunk, which is the cleanest possible evidence that the pool is global rather than shard-local:

![chunk consistency](results/runs/20260502_184314/figures/04_chunk_consistency.png)

### Finding 2 â€” Lexical overlap (IoU) is low â€” TF1 morals are *not* copy-pasted into fables

The pre-experiment worry was that Llama-3.1-8B, having been told the moral, would lexically embed it in the story. The data refutes this: TF1 mean IoU is 0.0218 (no-stopwords) â€” only 2Ã— MORABLES' 0.0107, and both well under 0.05.

![IoU distributions](results/runs/20260502_184314/figures/02_iou_distribution.png)

Reading the histogram:
- **MORABLES** has a huge spike at IoU=0 (median = 0.000) â€” many literary morals share *no* content words with their fable. This is the gold-standard hard-task distribution.
- **TF1** is shifted right but bounded at ~0.05. The distribution looks bimodal (moral-length artifact: short morals â†’ discrete IoU jumps), but the support stays in the same low-overlap regime as MORABLES.

**Implication:** TF1 is a legitimate moralâ†’fable retrieval signal, not a keyword-matching shortcut.

## Why TF1 cannot be a training-data replacement

`MultipleNegativesRankingLoss` (used in [`finetuning/lib/trainer.py:146`](../../finetuning/lib/trainer.py#L146)) assumes **one positive per anchor** and treats all other in-batch examples as negatives. With ~30,000 fables per moral in TF1, the probability that a batch of 32 contains two fables sharing the same moral is overwhelming â€” at batch size 32, â‰¥85% of batches collide. Each collision wrongly punishes a true positive as a negative, degrading training signal.

Three honest workarounds exist:
1. **Build moral-disjoint batches** â€” possible but kills shuffling efficiency.
2. **Multi-positive contrastive loss** (SupCon, multi-positive InfoNCE) â€” works, but is a non-trivial training-loop rewrite.
3. **Reframe as 100-way classification** â€” TF1 isn't really a retrieval dataset; it's labeled instances. Useful for some tasks, not ours.

---

## Why the upstream `tinyfabulist` generator is the right answer

[`tinyfabulist`](https://github.com/klusai/tinyfabulist) is the open-source pipeline that produced TF1-3M. The morals live in plain YAML at [`tinyfabulist/conf/generator_features.yaml`](https://github.com/klusai/tinyfabulist/blob/main/tinyfabulist/conf/generator_features.yaml) under `generator.features.morals:`, and the prompt template uses `{{moral}}` substitution. **One file edit** swaps in our 678 MORABLES morals.

Architecture: Python CLI â†’ OpenAI-compatible HTTP client â†’ any LLM backend (HF Inference Endpoints, vLLM, Ollama). Auth via `HF_ACCESS_TOKEN` or any OpenAI-style API key.

| Backend | Cost / Setup | When to use |
|---|---|---|
| Ollama (local Llama-3.1-8B-q4) | Free, slow (~20 s/fable) | 100-fable spike on laptop |
| vLLM on our GPU server | GPU-time only | Real scale (1Kâ€“50K fables) |
| HF Inference Endpoint | ~$0.14 / 1K fables | Cloud, matches the paper exactly |
| OpenAI / Anthropic | Per-token cost | Quality jump, loses paper-comparability |

This unlocks experiments TF1-3M cannot: **custom morals**, **controlled K fables per moral**, **prompt-template tuning** (age group, length, style).

## Recommendations â€” what to do with this finding

| Use | Verdict | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| Direct training-set replacement | **No** | Only 100 morals; loss collisions break contrastive training. |
| Hard-negative pool for MORABLES | **Yes** | Drop-in: 100 LLM-quality proverbs as distractors, far richer than the current 2,803 augmented set in [`data/processed/morals_augmented_corpus.json`](../../data/processed/morals_augmented_corpus.json). |
| Cross-domain generalization eval | **Yes (small)** | Test if MORABLES-trained models transfer to LLM-generated fables. Report Recall@k since the evaluation pool shape differs. |
| Pretraining via vanilla `MultipleNegativesRankingLoss` | **No** | See loss-collision analysis above. |
| Pretraining via multi-positive contrastive loss | **Conditional** | Worth it only if Spike A (below) shows quality is acceptable. |
| Generate custom synthetic fables seeded from MORABLES morals (via `tinyfabulist`) | **Yes â€” primary recommendation** | Bypass all TF1-3M limitations. ~$1 of compute for 7K targeted fables. |

## Future considerations

### Spike A â€” `tinyfabulist` end-to-end with MORABLES morals (highest priority)

Goal: produce a small (~100-fable) sample of synthetic fables generated from our 678 MORABLES morals, validate quality, and decide whether to scale.

Steps:
1. New experiment dir `experiments/12_tinyfabulist_spike/`
2. Pip-install or vendor `tinyfabulist`
3. Override `generator_features.yaml` with our morals
4. Backend: Ollama for spike â†’ vLLM for scale
5. Run check_iou.py against the output to confirm IoU stays low
6. Manually inspect 20 random samples â€” do the fables embody our (often more sophisticated) literary morals coherently?

Decision criterion: if mean IoU stays < 0.04 *and* manual inspection shows â‰¥80% on-target fables, scale to ~10â€“50 fables per moral (7Kâ€“34K total).

### Spike B â€” TF1 as hard negatives for MORABLES

Goal: integrate the 100 TF1 morals as in-batch hard negatives for the existing ft_01/ft_02 training runs.

Steps:
1. Pre-embed all 100 TF1 morals with the current model checkpoint.
2. For each MORABLES batch, retrieve the K most-similar TF1 morals (cosine) as additional negatives.
3. Train ft_01 (BGE-base) with K âˆˆ {1, 3, 5} TF1-negatives per anchor; compare MRR@10 vs the no-negatives baseline.

Risk: TF1 morals are stylistically different (shorter, more aphoristic) â€” they may be too easy to be useful negatives. Pre-experiment sanity check: compute mean cosine similarity between TF1 and MORABLES morals first.

### Spike C â€” Generalization probe

Goal: Test whether MORABLES-trained models transfer to LLM-generated fables for *the same* moral concepts.

Steps:
1. Identify the 20â€“30 TF1 morals semantically closest to MORABLES morals (cosine similarity over embeddings).
2. Sample 50 TF1 fables for each â†’ ~1.5K eval pairs.
3. Run inference with the existing ft_01 / ft_02 checkpoints; report MRR@10 against the TF1 sub-corpus.
4. Drop in ablation: how does MRR change as we restrict the eval to TF1 morals that are *not* in MORABLES?

Publishable framing either way: "literaryâ†’synthetic transfer in moral retrieval."

### Open questions worth flagging

1. **Are the 100 morals listed somewhere in the paper?** Worth checking the appendix. If yes, we can directly correlate them to MORABLES morals semantically.
2. **What's the in-set diversity of TF1 fables for the same moral?** Two fables sharing the moral "greed leads to downfall" â€” how different are they? Cosine over embeddings would tell us.
3. **Does TF1 break at very long-tail morals?** Our MORABLES set has unusual literary morals (e.g., *"Vicious people cannot resist hurting others even when it is not in their own interests"*). Can Llama-3.1-8B render fables coherent for these?
